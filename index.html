So, when I happened to read an articleby Nicole Spector about how smiling can trick one's brain into happiness, i felt different yet again.

First, the article argues that happiness is not a prerequisite to smiling; in fact,smiling can be used as a tool to generate happieness. if one smiles, the brain assume that

there musthave been an amusing event that triggered the responce. However, the brain does not.To put it in Spector's words,"the brain is a sucker for grin" and it does not care whether one is genuinely smiling or just faking it.Spector corroborates this with credible studies.For exp, at the university of Cardiff in wales,researches found that people who were unable to frown due to botox injections were happier than the normal ones who could frown. The second cornerstone of this article talks about why a smile is contagious, just like a yawn. reason: mirror neurons. At a mechanical level,mirror neurons enable one to reflect the other's behavior. Hence, when one smiles,another person sees the smike and cannot help but also smile. This means that mirror neurons were immediately triggered and the person smiled.At a deeper emotional level,one reciprocates the smile because she feels endered and recieve positive vibrations, both of which one would want to pass on to others.
